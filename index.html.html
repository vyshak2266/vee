Mercury:

Closest to the Sun
Smallest planet
No atmosphere
extreme temperature fluctuations Rocky surface

Venus:

Similar size to Earth
Thick, toxic atmosphere rich in carbon dioxide
Hottest planet due to greenhouse effect
No moons

Earth:

The only planet known to support life
Has liquid water
Atmosphere composed of nitrogen and oxygen
One moon

Mars

Known as the Red Planet due to iron oxide on its surface
Thin atmosphere, mostly carbon dioxide
Evidence of ancient water flow
Two small moons (Phobos and Deimos)

Jupiter:

Largest planet in the Solar System
Gas giant made mostly of hydrogen and helium
Famous for its Great Red Spot, a massive storm Over 79 moons, including Ganymede (largest in the Solar System)


Saturn:

Known for its prominent ring system
Gas giant, mostly hydrogen and helium
Over 80 moons, including Titan, which has a thick atmosphere


Uranus:

Ice giant with a blue-green color due to methane in the atmosphere
Rotates on its side (unique axial tilt)
Coldest planet
27 known moons


Neptune:

Farthest planet from the Sun
Ice giant, deep blue color
Strongest winds in the Solar System
14 known moons, with Triton being the largest
